The Position
NOT A
TRAINING
TOOL.

Security awareness training operates on a false premise: that repeated instruction moves people toward safer behavior. It doesn't — because behavior is not the root problem.

Behavior is the output of stable traits interacting with variable conditions. A person with high social compliance doesn't become less compliant because they completed a phishing module. Their compliance is a relational orientation — a stable architecture. What changes is whether the conditions they're operating in are actively exploiting it.

This distinction changes everything about what a useful security instrument should do. It shouldn't try to train the trait. It should map the trait accurately, monitor the conditions continuously, and signal when their intersection becomes exploitable.

That's Veros. Not a training platform. A trait-relative risk instrument — built on the recognition that knowing the architecture of someone's vulnerability is more valuable than trying to rebuild it.

The Instrument
THE IBRI

The Intangybl Behavioral Risk Index. Four dimensions. Each one maps a stable trait architecture — not a behavior score. Not a training outcome. A precise instrument reading of how a person is built to respond.

DIMENSION 01
FV
Fatigue Vulnerability
The rate and severity at which cognitive depletion degrades decision quality. High FV individuals show rapid instinct suppression under load — verification steps feel costly, urgency pressure feels legitimate.
Most situational of the four. Trait-level stress tolerance determines the floor — conditions determine the expression.
DIMENSION 02
SC
Social Compliance
A relational orientation — a stable tendency to weight authority, belonging, and others' expectations heavily in decision-making. Amplifies in hierarchical environments and under perceived urgency from trusted sources.
Cannot be trained away. Can be mapped precisely — so the conditions that activate it can be monitored and flagged.
DIMENSION 03
RR
Risk Rationalization
A cognitive style — the stable tendency to construct post-hoc justifications for decisions already made intuitively. Amplifies under high cognitive load. Makes over-explained, plausible-seeming requests more dangerous, not less.
Attackers who understand RR don't hide their rationale — they over-supply it. The more thorough the explanation, the higher the risk for high-RR profiles.
DIMENSION 04
EA
Exposure Awareness
The closest to a knowledge and habit dimension — familiarity with attack patterns, domain verification instincts, and established security practice. The most trainable of the four. Where conventional security education actually works.
Veros treats EA differently from the other three — because the mechanism of improvement is genuinely different here.
◈
The IBRI score is not a fixed label. It's a continuous reading of trait expression under current conditions. The same person scores differently when their environment shifts — not because their architecture changed, but because different conditions activate different dimensions at different intensities. The architecture is what Veros maps. The conditions are what Veros watches.
The System
HOW VEROS
OPERATES
01
Profile the Architecture
Veros builds a behavioral profile through self-reported scenario responses — not passive observation. The result is an IBRI baseline across all four dimensions: a precise map of how this person is built to respond under different conditions.
Consent-based · Self-reported
02
Monitor the Conditions
The Pulse layer collects daily environmental signal through minimal check-ins — three questions, one tap each, embedded in existing workflow. When conditions shift the profile toward exploitable territory, the Drift Engine detects it and surfaces a signal.
Daily signal · Drift detection
03
Deploy at Threshold
When drift exceeds baseline parameters, a calibrated scenario deploys — built from the current profile state, not a generic template. The debrief reveals exactly which dimensions were targeted and how the current conditions made them accessible. The IBRI updates.
Profile-driven · Forensic debrief
